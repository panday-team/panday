# Electrician Common Core Level 2

Level 2 technical training advances your electrical education with **10 weeks (300 hours) of intensive instruction** focused on single-phase AC systems, commercial and industrial installations, and increasingly complex electrical applications. You'll dive deeper into transformer theory, renewable energy systems, HVAC controls, and advanced motor starters.

This remains part of the **Common Core** curriculum shared by both Construction and Industrial Electrician pathwaysâ€”specialization still doesn't occur until Level 4.

## Before You Begin

**Work Experience Requirement**:
You'll need approximately **2,300 cumulative hours total** before Level 2 technical training (roughly 1,500 additional hours after completing Level 1), typically requiring another year of full-time employment.

**Prerequisites**:
- Successfully completed Level 1 technical training
- Passed the Level 1 standardized exam (minimum 70%)
- Logged required work-based hours through SkilledTradesBC portal
- Employer sign-off confirming competency development
- Maintained good standing with SkilledTradesBC

## Technical Training Curriculum

The 10-week Level 2 program covers single-phase AC systems and commercial/industrial applications:

### Single-Phase AC Circuits - Inductance, Capacitance, Impedance, Power Factor (41% of curriculum)
- AC fundamentals and waveform analysis
- Inductive and capacitive reactance
- Impedance calculations in AC circuits
- Power factor concepts and correction
- True power, reactive power, and apparent power
- Phasor diagrams and AC circuit analysis

### Commercial and Industrial Drawings (5%)
- Reading commercial blueprints and specifications
- Industrial electrical drawings interpretation
- One-line diagrams and riser diagrams
- Panel schedules and equipment layouts
- Specifications and submittals

### Single-Phase Services with Current Transformers (5%)
- Large single-phase services
- Current transformer (CT) applications
- CT-rated meter installations
- Service sizing for commercial loads
- Demand calculations

### Overcurrent Protection and Surge Protection (4%)
- Circuit breaker types and applications
- Fuse classification and coordination
- Surge protective devices (SPDs)
- Transient voltage surge suppression
- Selective coordination principles

### Single-Phase AC Grounding (2%)
- Advanced grounding systems for commercial installations
- Separately derived systems
- Grounding transformers
- Ground fault protection

### Renewable Energy Basics (2%)
- Solar photovoltaic system fundamentals
- Wind generation principles
- Fuel cell technology introduction
- Grid-tied vs. off-grid systems
- Renewable energy code requirements

### Single-Phase Transformers, Polarity, Connections, Instrument Transformers (13%)
- Transformer construction and operating principles
- Voltage and current transformers
- Polarity testing and additive/subtractive polarity
- Transformer connections and phasing
- Instrument transformers (PT and CT)
- Transformer calculations and sizing

### Commercial and Industrial Conductors (2%)
- Large conductor sizing and selection
- Parallel conductor installations
- Derating factors for commercial applications
- Cable tray systems
- Bus duct and busway systems

### Advanced Luminaires and Lighting Controls (6%)
- Commercial lighting systems
- LED technology and drivers
- Lighting control systems
- Occupancy sensors and daylight harvesting
- Emergency lighting requirements

### HVAC Systems and Controls (6%)
- Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning electrical requirements
- HVAC control circuits
- Thermostat wiring
- Contactors and relays for HVAC
- Variable speed drives for HVAC applications

### Exit and Emergency Lighting (3%)
- Life safety code requirements
- Exit sign installation and testing
- Emergency lighting units
- Battery backup systems
- Monthly and annual testing procedures

### Cathodic Protection (1%)
- Corrosion prevention fundamentals
- Impressed current systems
- Sacrificial anode systems
- Applications for underground infrastructure

### Magnetic Motor Starters and Controls (10%)
- Three-wire control circuits
- Start-stop-jog circuits
- Forward-reverse motor control
- Multiple motor control stations
- Interlocking and sequencing
- Control transformers and pilot devices

## Passing Requirements

To successfully complete Level 2:

- **Minimum 70%** on in-school assessments
- **Minimum 70%** on the standardized Level 2 exam
- Satisfactory completion of all practical assignments

## Financial Support During Training

**Employment Insurance (EI) Benefits**:
- Same as Level 1: Up to **$668 per week** for the full 10 weeks
- Apply 4 weeks before training through [WorkBC Apprentice Services](https://workbcapprenticeservices.ca)

**Canada Apprentice Loan**:
- Up to **$4,000 per period** (permanently interest-free)
- Can borrow for each level of training
- Maximum $20,000 total across all levels

## Work-Based Training After Level 2

**Progression to Level 3**:
You'll need approximately **4,500 cumulative hours total** before Level 3 technical training (typically 1,500-2,000 additional hours after Level 2), representing approximately three years into your apprenticeship.

**Supervision and Hour Logging**:
- Continue working under certified journeyperson (2:1 apprentice ratio maximum)
- Employers log hours through SkilledTradesBC Portal every 3-6 months
- Increased earning potential: Typically 50-70% of journeyperson wage at this level

## What Comes Next

Upon completing Level 2, you'll:

1. **Progress to Level 3**: Learn three-phase power systems, advanced motors, transformers, and generating systems
2. **Higher Earning Potential**: Wage increases with demonstrated competency
3. **More Complex Responsibilities**: Take on commercial and industrial installations
4. **Specialization Approaching**: After Level 3, you'll choose Construction or Industrial pathway at Level 4
