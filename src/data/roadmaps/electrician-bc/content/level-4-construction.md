# Construction Electrician Level 4

Level 4 Construction is your **specialization pathway focused on building electrical systems**—commercial construction, residential installations, fire alarms, security systems, and building automation. This is where you diverge from the Industrial stream and develop expertise in the electrical systems that power buildings and infrastructure.

You'll complete **10 weeks (300 hours) of specialized technical training** followed by approximately **1,500 final work-based hours**, bringing you to **6,000 cumulative hours**—ready to challenge the Red Seal Construction Electrician exam and earn journeyperson status.

## Before You Begin

**Work Experience Requirement**:
Approximately **6,000 cumulative hours total** before Red Seal certification (1,500 additional hours after Level 3), representing approximately four years of full-time employment.

**Prerequisites**:
- Completed Levels 1, 2, and 3 technical training
- Passed all level exams (70% minimum)
- Logged 4,500 work-based hours
- **Chosen Construction specialization** in consultation with employer
- Good standing with SkilledTradesBC

## Technical Training Curriculum (Construction-Specific)

Level 4 Construction focuses on building systems and construction site installations:

### Advanced Electronic Applications (20% of curriculum)
- Programmable logic controllers (PLCs) for building systems
- PLC programming fundamentals
- Human-machine interfaces (HMIs)
- Building automation system integration
- Electronic control circuits for construction applications
- Microprocessor-based controls

### Interpreting Drawings and Identifying Hazardous Locations (7%)
- Complex commercial and institutional drawings
- Hazardous location classifications (Class I, II, III; Division 1, 2)
- Hazardous location wiring methods
- Explosion-proof equipment selection
- Gas station and paint booth installations
- Coordination with architectural and mechanical drawings

### Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) Systems (4%)
- UPS operating principles and types
- Standby, line-interactive, and double-conversion UPS
- Battery backup systems
- Critical loads and emergency power
- UPS sizing and installation
- Maintenance and testing

### Renewable Energy Systems (8%)
- **Solar PV systems**: Design, installation, code requirements
- **Wind generation**: Small-scale turbine installations
- **Energy storage**: Battery systems, grid-tied storage
- Net metering and utility interconnection
- Solar combiner boxes and rapid shutdown
- Renewable energy code compliance (CEC Section 84)

### High Voltage Systems Installation and Maintenance (10%)
- High-voltage equipment and safety
- Medium-voltage switchgear
- Pad-mounted transformers
- Cable terminations and splicing
- High-voltage testing procedures
- Lockout/tagout for high-voltage equipment

### Specialized Branch Circuitry (4%)
- **Airport runway lighting** systems
- **Traffic signal** installations
- **Street lighting** controls
- Specialized outdoor lighting
- Photocell and time clock controls
- Municipal infrastructure electrical systems

### Non-Rotating Equipment (2%)
- Electric heating systems (baseboard, radiant, forced air)
- Water heaters and boilers
- Infrared heaters
- Heat trace systems
- Swimming pool and spa electrical requirements

### Fire Alarms, Security, Surveillance Signaling Systems (8%)
- Fire alarm system design and installation
- Addressable vs. conventional systems
- Smoke detectors, heat detectors, pull stations
- Notification appliances (strobes, horns)
- Fire alarm control panels
- **Security systems**: Access control, intrusion detection
- **Surveillance**: CCTV camera installations and NVRs
- Integration with building management systems

### Voice-Data-Video (VDV) Systems and Nurse Call Communication Systems (6%)
- Structured cabling systems (Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6a)
- Cable pathway systems and cable management
- Termination and testing of data cables
- Fiber optic cable basics
- **Nurse call systems** for healthcare facilities
- Communication riser systems
- Low-voltage communication infrastructure

### Building Automation Systems (6%)
- Building management system (BMS) fundamentals
- HVAC controls integration
- Lighting control systems
- Energy management and monitoring
- BACnet and Modbus protocols
- DDC (Direct Digital Control) systems

### PLCs and Programming for Automated Control Systems (25%)
- **This is the largest component of Level 4 Construction**
- Advanced PLC programming techniques
- Ladder logic, function block diagrams
- Timers, counters, and sequencing
- Analog inputs and outputs
- PID control loops
- Troubleshooting PLC systems
- Safety PLCs and interlocks
- Industrial communication protocols
- SCADA system basics

## Work Environments

As a Construction Electrician, you'll work in:

- **Residential buildings**: Houses, townhomes, condominiums
- **Commercial buildings**: Offices, retail, restaurants, hotels
- **Institutional facilities**: Schools, hospitals, government buildings
- **Transportation systems**: Airports, transit stations, rail facilities
- **Marine environments**: Docks, marinas, shipyards
- **Entertainment venues**: Theaters, stadiums, arenas
- **New construction and renovation projects**

## Career Characteristics

**Project-Based Work**:
- Variety in worksites and projects (every building is different)
- Typically work for electrical contractors
- Move between job sites as projects complete
- Opportunity to see completed buildings you helped wire

**Employment Patterns**:
- Often seasonal (busier in spring/summer/fall)
- May experience layoffs between projects
- Strong demand in growing urban areas
- Economic cycles affect construction starts

**Entrepreneurship Opportunities**:
- **Start your own electrical contracting business**
- Residential service and renovation work
- Small commercial contracts
- Build client base and reputation
- Master Electrician license requirements vary by municipality

**Wage Expectations**:
- Journeyperson wages: **$34-$50/hour** depending on region and employer
- Union construction electricians often earn higher rates
- Overtime common during busy periods
- Benefits vary by employer (union vs. non-union)

## Can You Switch to Industrial Later?

**Yes, with cross-credit!**
- Construction Electrician certificate holders receive credit for Levels 1-3 and 4,500 work hours
- Must complete only **Level 4 Industrial** training
- Wait until harmonized Level 4 is fully implemented
- Employer must support the transition

## What Comes Next

Upon completing Level 4 Construction:

1. **Finish Work Hours**: Reach 6,000 cumulative hours
2. **Apply for Red Seal Exam**: Submit application through SkilledTradesBC Portal
3. **Study for Red Seal**: 3-6 month preparation recommended
4. **Write Red Seal Exam**: 100-question, 4-hour exam (70% to pass)
5. **Earn Journeyperson Status**: BC Certificate of Qualification with Red Seal endorsement
6. **Work Anywhere in Canada**: Interprovincial recognition
