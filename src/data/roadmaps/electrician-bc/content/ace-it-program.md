# ACE IT Program (Accelerated Credit Enrolment in Industry Training)

ACE IT is a specialized program within BC's Youth Work in Trades system that allows high school students to gain a **massive head start** on their electrical apprenticeshipâ€”earning **up to 16 graduation credits plus 480 work-based apprenticeship hours** while receiving wages and completing Grade 12.

This is the ultimate early-entry pathway: you can complete foundation or Level 1 technical training at post-secondary institutions while still in high school, register as an official SkilledTradesBC apprentice, and graduate with both your Dogwood Diploma and significant progress toward Red Seal certification.

## What Is ACE IT?

ACE IT is a model within Youth Work in Trades programs that enables students to:

**Complete Post-Secondary Training While in High School**:
- Attend foundation or Level 1 technical training at BC colleges (BCIT, Camosun, VIU, etc.)
- Training aligns with college intake schedules
- Must register with SkilledTradesBC during Level 1 training

**Earn Dual Credentials Simultaneously**:
- High school graduation credits through WRK courses (WRK 11A, WRK 11B, WRK 12A, WRK 12B)
- SkilledTradesBC apprenticeship credentials
- Foundation program or Level 1 completion certificates

**Receive Financial Support**:
- ACE IT funding may cover tuition costs for post-secondary training
- Youth Work in Trades Scholarship available for ongoing trades training
- Wages earned during work-based hours

## Eligibility

To participate in ACE IT, you must:

- **Be Currently Enrolled** in a BC high school (typically Grades 10-12)
- **Secure an Employer Willing to Sponsor** your apprenticeship
- **Have School District Support** - Your school must offer ACE IT programming for the electrician trade
- **Complete WRK Courses**: WRK 11A, WRK 11B, WRK 12A, and WRK 12B coursework

**Check with your school guidance counselor** to determine if your school district offers ACE IT for electrician trades. Not all districts offer all trade programs.

## Registration Process

1. **Talk to Your Guidance Counselor**
   - Confirm ACE IT availability for electrician trade at your school
   - Understand the WRK course sequence and timing
   - Get information about participating post-secondary institutions

2. **Find an Employer Sponsor**
   - Work with your school's trades coordinator who often has employer connections
   - Attend youth trades job fairs and networking events
   - Employers benefit from tax credits and developing future skilled workers

3. **Register as a Youth Apprentice**
   - Submit the Youth Apprentice and Sponsor Registration Form at [SkilledTradesBC Forms](https://forms.skilledtradesbc.ca/wbtform)
   - Both student and sponsor must sign the form
   - Registration makes you an official apprentice

4. **Enroll in Technical Training**
   - Apply to the post-secondary institution offering foundation or Level 1 training
   - Coordinate timing with your school schedule and college intakes
   - ACE IT funding covers tuition for eligible students

5. **Complete Work-Based Hours**
   - Accumulate 480+ apprenticeship work hours while still in high school
   - Earn wages during these hours
   - Hours count toward your official apprenticeship progression

6. **Complete WRK Courses**
   - Earn up to 16 high school graduation credits
   - Credits count toward Dogwood Diploma

## Financial Benefits

**Youth Work in Trades Scholarship**:
- Application-based funding for post-secondary trades training tuition and mandatory fees
- Available for students registered as youth apprentices from July 1, 2024 onward
- Application period: September 2 to November 14, 4:00 PM PST annually
- Apply at [WRK Scholarship](https://www2.gov.bc.ca/gov/content/education-training/k-12/support/career-programs/wrk-scholarship)
- Email: WRK.Scholarships@gov.bc.ca

**ACE IT Tuition Coverage**:
- ACE IT funding may cover tuition costs for post-secondary technical training
- Check with your school district and institution for specific funding details

**Wages During Work Hours**:
- Earn apprentice wages for your 480+ work hours
- All work hours accumulate toward official apprenticeship requirements
- Paid work experience while completing high school

## What You'll Accomplish

By graduation, ACE IT participants will have:

**Educational Credits**:
- **High school diploma** with up to 16 graduation credits from WRK courses
- **Foundation program or Level 1 certificate** from post-secondary institution
- **SkilledTradesBC registration** as an official apprentice

**Work Experience**:
- **480+ work-based apprenticeship hours** already accumulated
- Wages earned during work hours
- Industry connections and employer relationships

**Career Advancement**:
- Enter the apprenticeship system at Level 1 or Level 2 (if foundation completed)
- 6-12 months ahead of traditional post-high-school entrants
- Proven track record to employers

## After High School: What Comes Next

Upon graduating high school, ACE IT participants can:

1. **Continue with Your Sponsor**: If your employer sponsor continues your employment, you seamlessly progress through the apprenticeship
2. **Find New Sponsor**: Your credentials and hours transfer to any new employer sponsor
3. **Attend Next Level Training**: Proceed to Level 2 or Level 3 technical training based on accumulated hours
4. **Progress to Red Seal**: Continue the standard pathway toward Construction or Industrial Electrician Red Seal certification

You're already 480-1,500 hours ahead of your peers who start after high school, potentially saving 6-12 months on your journey to certification.
