# Industrial Electrician Level 4

Level 4 Industrial is your **specialization pathway focused on industrial plant electrical systems**—manufacturing equipment, process control, PLCs, SCADA, motor maintenance, and industrial automation. This is where you diverge from the Construction stream and develop expertise in keeping industrial facilities running 24/7.

You'll complete **10 weeks (300 hours) of specialized technical training** followed by approximately **1,500 final work-based hours**, bringing you to **6,000 cumulative hours**—ready to challenge the Red Seal Industrial Electrician exam and earn journeyperson status.

## Before You Begin

**Work Experience Requirement**:
Approximately **6,000 cumulative hours total** before Red Seal certification (1,500 additional hours after Level 3), representing approximately four years of full-time employment in industrial settings.

**Prerequisites**:
- Completed Levels 1, 2, and 3 technical training
- Passed all level exams (70% minimum)
- Logged 4,500 work-based hours
- **Chosen Industrial specialization** in consultation with employer
- Good standing with SkilledTradesBC

## Technical Training Curriculum (Industrial-Specific)

Level 4 Industrial focuses on industrial plant systems, automation, and maintenance:

### Industrial Electronic Applications (20% of curriculum)
- Industrial control circuits and electronics
- Variable frequency drives (VFDs) for industrial applications
- Soft starters and motor protection
- DC drives and servo systems
- Process control instrumentation
- Industrial sensors and transducers

### Industrial Drawings and Hazardous Locations (7%)
- P&IDs (Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams)
- Loop diagrams and instrument schedules
- **Hazardous location** classifications for industrial environments
- Class I, II, III; Division 1, 2; Zone classifications
- Intrinsically safe systems
- Explosion-proof and purged enclosures

### Industrial Power Conditioning and UPS (4%)
- Power conditioning for sensitive industrial equipment
- Harmonic filtering and mitigation
- Industrial UPS systems for process control
- Battery backup for critical systems
- Power factor correction equipment
- Voltage regulation and stabilization

### Industrial Renewable Applications (8%)
- Industrial-scale solar installations
- Cogeneration systems
- Waste-to-energy systems
- Industrial energy storage
- Grid integration for industrial facilities
- Demand response and load management

### Industrial High Voltage Systems (10%)
- Industrial high-voltage distribution
- Medium-voltage motor starters
- High-voltage switchgear and breakers
- Cable fault location and testing
- Arc flash hazard analysis
- Electrical safety in industrial environments

### Under/Over Voltage Protection Devices, Industrial Pumps, Heaters, Specialized Non-Rotating Equipment (2%)
- **Voltage monitoring relays** and protection devices
- **Industrial pump systems**: Process pumps, sump pumps, boiler feed pumps
- **Industrial heaters**: Process heaters, tank heaters, immersion heaters
- **Specialized equipment**: Compressors, chillers, industrial ovens
- Equipment control and protection

### Industrial Fire Alarms and Security (8%)
- Industrial fire detection and suppression systems
- Gas detection systems (combustible, toxic gases)
- Emergency shutdown systems (ESD)
- Industrial security and access control
- Intrusion detection for critical areas
- Integration with plant safety systems

### Industrial VDV Communication Systems (6%)
- Industrial Ethernet and fiber optics
- Plant-wide communication networks
- Industrial wireless systems
- Device networking (DeviceNet, Profibus, EtherNet/IP)
- Industrial telephony and paging
- Remote monitoring and diagnostics

### Industrial Automation (6%)
- Distributed control systems (DCS)
- Supervisory control and data acquisition (**SCADA**)
- Manufacturing execution systems (MES)
- Industrial IoT (IIoT) applications
- Predictive maintenance systems
- Energy management systems

### PLCs, SCADA, HMI, Programming, and Optimization for Automated Control (25%)
- **This is the largest component of Level 4 Industrial**
- Advanced PLC programming for industrial processes
- Allen-Bradley, Siemens, Modicon platforms
- SCADA system design and implementation
- HMI (Human-Machine Interface) design and configuration
- Process optimization and tuning
- PID loop tuning for process control
- Batch processing and recipe management
- Safety-rated PLCs (SIL-rated systems)
- Industrial communication protocols (Modbus, Profinet, OPC)
- Troubleshooting complex automated systems

### Pneumatic/Hydraulic Fluid Power Systems (Industrial-Specific Emphasis)
- **Unique to Industrial stream**
- Pneumatic system components and circuits
- Hydraulic system components and circuits
- Electro-pneumatic controls
- Electro-hydraulic controls
- Fluid power troubleshooting
- Integration with electrical control systems

## Work Environments

As an Industrial Electrician, you'll work in:

- **Manufacturing plants**: Automotive, aerospace, electronics, food processing
- **Mines and smelters**: Underground mining, ore processing, metal refinement
- **Pulp and paper mills**: Continuous process operations
- **Shipyards**: Ship construction and repair facilities
- **Oil and gas operations**: Refineries, offshore platforms, pipeline facilities
- **Hydroelectric dams and power generation**: Utilities and power plants
- **Industrial facilities**: Chemical plants, pharmaceutical manufacturing

## Career Characteristics

**Full-Time Plant Positions**:
- Steady year-round employment with single employer
- Typically permanent positions in maintenance departments
- Job security tied to plant operations
- Become expert in your facility's systems

**Shift Work**:
- **Premium pay** for evening, night, and weekend shifts
- Rotating shifts (days, afternoons, nights) common in 24/7 operations
- Shift differential pay (e.g., +10% for afternoons, +15% for nights)
- Work-life balance considerations with shift schedules

**Maintenance Focus**:
- **Troubleshooting and repair** emphasis (not new installations)
- Preventive and predictive maintenance programs
- Emergency repairs to minimize downtime
- Deep technical knowledge of specific equipment

**Deep Specialization**:
- Become expert in **industrial controls** and automation
- PLC programming and SCADA systems
- Motor repair and predictive maintenance
- High-voltage electrical systems
- Process control and instrumentation

**Wage Expectations**:
- Journeyperson wages: **$34-$50/hour** base rate
- **Shift premiums** add 10-15% for off-hours
- Overtime common during maintenance shutdowns
- Comprehensive benefits typical (union or direct with employer)
- Pension plans common in larger industrial facilities

## Can You Switch to Construction Later?

**Yes, with cross-credit!**
- Industrial Electrician certificate holders receive credit for Levels 1-3 and 4,500 work hours
- Must complete only **Level 4 Construction** training
- Wait until harmonized Level 4 is fully implemented
- Employer must support the transition

## What Comes Next

Upon completing Level 4 Industrial:

1. **Finish Work Hours**: Reach 6,000 cumulative hours
2. **Apply for Red Seal Exam**: Submit application through SkilledTradesBC Portal
3. **Study for Red Seal**: 3-6 month preparation recommended
4. **Write Red Seal Exam**: 100-question, 4-hour exam (70% to pass)
5. **Earn Journeyperson Status**: BC Certificate of Qualification with Red Seal endorsement
6. **Work Anywhere in Canada**: Interprovincial recognition
